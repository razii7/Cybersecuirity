## Overview

This module introduced the fundamentals of computer networking, including different network types, how data is represented and transmitted, and the concepts of bandwidth and throughput.

Networking enables devices worldwide to communicate and exchange information. Modern life relies heavily on networks for communication, education, business, entertainment, and smart devices.

---

## 1. Network Types

### What is a Network?

A network is a group of interconnected devices that can communicate and share data and resources.

### Key Concepts

#### The Internet

The Internet is a global collection of interconnected networks that allows communication and information sharing worldwide.

#### Who Owns the Internet?

No single person, company, or government owns the Internet. It operates through cooperation among Internet service providers, organizations, governments, and standards bodies.

#### Local Networks (LAN)

A Local Area Network (LAN) connects devices within a limited geographic area such as:

* Home
* School
* Office
* Campus

#### Mobile Networks

Mobile networks allow smartphones and tablets to connect through cellular technologies such as 4G and 5G.

#### Connected Home Devices

Examples include:

* Smart TVs
* Smart speakers
* Security cameras
* Smart lights
* Smart appliances

These devices form part of the Internet of Things (IoT).

#### Other Connected Devices

Many modern devices use networking capabilities, including:

* Vehicles
* Wearable devices
* Medical equipment
* Industrial systems

## 2. Data Transmission

### Personal Data

Personal data includes information that identifies or relates to an individual, such as:

* Name
* Address
* Phone number
* Email address
* Financial information

Protecting personal data is an important aspect of cybersecurity.

### The Bit

The bit is the smallest unit of digital information.

A bit can have only two possible values:

* 0
* 1

All digital communication is ultimately represented using combinations of bits.

### Common Methods of Data Transmission

Data can be transmitted through:

#### Wired Media

* Ethernet cables
* Fiber-optic cables

#### Wireless Media

* Wi-Fi
* Bluetooth
* Cellular networks

The choice of transmission medium affects speed, reliability, and range.

---

## 3. Bandwidth and Throughput

### Bandwidth

Bandwidth refers to the maximum amount of data that can be transmitted over a network connection within a given time.

Bandwidth is typically measured in:

* bits per second (bps)
* Kbps
* Mbps
* Gbps

### Throughput

Throughput refers to the actual amount of data successfully transmitted over the network during a given period.

### Difference Between Bandwidth and Throughput

| Bandwidth         | Throughput         |
| ----------------- | ------------------ |
| Maximum capacity  | Actual performance |
| Theoretical value | Real-world value   |
| Usually higher    | Usually lower      |

Example:

A network connection may have a bandwidth of 100 Mbps, but due to congestion and interference, the actual throughput may be only 80 Mbps.

## Key Takeaways

* Networks connect devices and enable communication.
* The Internet is a global network of interconnected networks.
* Local Area Networks (LANs) connect devices in limited areas.
* Mobile and IoT devices rely heavily on networking technologies.
* Digital information is represented using bits.
* Data can be transmitted through wired and wireless media.
* Bandwidth represents maximum network capacity.
* Throughput represents actual network performance.
